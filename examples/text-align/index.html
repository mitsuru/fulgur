Text Alignment Examples

1. Left (default)

Left-aligned text is the default for LTR languages. Lines start from the left margin and have a ragged right edge.

2. Center

Center-aligned text is commonly used for headings and short blocks. Each line is centered within the container.

3. Right

Right-aligned text starts from the right margin. It is used for dates, signatures, and certain design elements.

4. Justify

Justified text stretches each line to fill the full width of the container, creating even left and right edges. This style is often used in books, newspapers, and formal documents to give a clean, polished appearance. The last line of a justified paragraph is typically left-aligned.
