Text Decoration Examples

1. Decoration Lines

Underline — the most common text decoration

Overline — placed above the text

Line-through — used for deleted or outdated content

Underline + Line-through — multiple lines combined

2. Decoration Styles

Solid (default) — a simple straight line

Dashed — a dashed line pattern

Dotted — a dotted line pattern

Double — two parallel lines

Wavy — a wavy line, often used for spelling errors

3. Decoration Colors

Red underline — highlighting errors or warnings

Blue underline — indicating links or references

Green underline — marking approved or correct items

Red wavy — the classic spell-check indicator

Blue double — emphatic reference styling

4. Inline Usage

Regular text with an underlined phrase in the middle of a sentence, followed by a struck-through word and then normal text again.

Mixing colored underline with colored wavy in the same paragraph.
